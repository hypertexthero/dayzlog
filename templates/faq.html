Day Z?

Day Z is a modification (mod) of a men-with-guns simulation called Arma 2. It is arguably the game that the relatively tedious Arma 2 simulation was missing. Arma 2 provides large maps with forests and urban areas, accurate ballistic physics, good open-area sound simulation, day, night and weather cycles, and many, thankfully virtual, guns. Day Z provides survival gameplay (hide, scavenge, hunt), flares, chemlights, axes, firewood, beans, multiple human-controlled players and a computer-controlled, thankfully virtual, zombie horde. The wild popularity of Day Z means that a standalone game is being developed.

To play Day Z (Windows-only, unfortunately) you currently need:

  1. A copy of the imaginatively-named Arma 2: Combined Ops.
  2. The Day Z mod. There are installation instructions at Day Z Wiki.

Day Z Log?

dayzlog.com is a website for people who have experienced Day Z’s human-zombie apocalypse — also known as ‘survivors’ — to share their stories on the web and in-game. After signing up for an account, survivors can:

  1. Write log entries
  2. Display profile information on the player roster in-game. Survivors do this by editing their profile on dayzlog.com and pasting a generated URL to their in-game Day Z settings
  3. Follow other survivor’s logs’ and vote on their entries. The homepage shows the top 30

My Day Z in-game player name: how do I find it?

  1. Start Day Z, go to the main menu and click on ‘Player profile’
  2. Your player profile name is listed here. Write it down. If you want to change it, select your player profile name and click ‘Edit’
  3. Write your desired player name in the Player name field and click ‘Okay’. Remember to {% if user.is_authenticated %}update your profile{% else %}update your profile{% endif %} with this exact player name in order to correctly display your profile information within Day Z.

My Day Z in-game player id: how do I find it?

  1. Start Day Z, go to the main menu and click on ‘Player profile’
  2. Select your player profile name and click ‘Edit’
